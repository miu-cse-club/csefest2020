Speaker Name

Shah Abdul Hannan

Former Secretary
Government of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh

Shah Abdul Hannan is a Bangladeshi Islamic philosopher, writer, economist, educator and media personality. He served as the deputy governor of the Bangladesh Bank and chairman of the National Board of Revenue. He was the founder vice chancellor of Darul Ihsan University, North South University, chairman of Board Of Trustees Of Manarat International University & Islamic Economics Research Bureau and the director of Islami Bank Bangladesh Ltd.

Speaker Name

Sadman Sadik

Chief Content Creator
Robi 10 Minute School

Sadman Sadik is an online educator and a best-selling author. Currently he's acting as the Chief Content Creator of the largest online education platform of Bangladesh 10 Minute School. He has worked with corporate clients like Bangladesh Brand Forum, Robi, IDLC, IPDC, Rokomari etc. He's one of the brand ambassadors of Samsung Mobile Bangladesh. He has published 4 books so far and has conducted training sessions in top educational institutions of Bangladesh.

Speaker Name

Mohammad Kaykobad

Computer scientist, educator, author, and columnist
Professor of computer science and engineering in BUET

Mohammad Kaykobad is a computer scientist, educator, author, and columnist from Bangladesh. Along with Muhammed Zafar Iqbal, he started the national mathematics olympiad. Currently he is a professor of computer science and engineering in Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology.

Speaker Name

Mahedi Hasan

Machine Learning Researcher and University lecturer
Research Interest: Deep Learning, Computer Vision
Manarat International University

Md Mahedi Hasan is currently pursuing his M.Sc in Institute of Information and Communication Technology (IICT) from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). Before that, he completed his B.Sc in EEE at Khulna University of Engineering and Technology (KUET). His research interest includes Machine Learning, Deep Unsupervised Learning, and Computer Vision and is currently focusing on action recognition and video understanding. In recent years, he has been involved in several research projects and authored several refereed journals and conference papers. He is also working as a Lecturer at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) in Manarat International University (MIU) since May 2019, where taught and delivered several courses, seminars, and workshops on neural network, computer vision and pattern recognition. He loves reading, traveling around, and problem-solving.

Math Olympiad Rules

In MIU CSE Fest 2020 we are going to organize Math Olympiad(Olny for college students). All ollege students of Bangladesh are requested to participate in this competition.

Rules of the Olympiad:
  1. Each student has to participate individually
  2. Question type: MCQ and Short-answer and all the questions will carry equal marks.
  3. Exam time 50 minutes.
  4. Be fair and honest, Plagiarism will result in disqualification
  5. Judge’s decisions will be final.

N.B.-The Competition is only amongst University Students and College Students. School level students are not allowed to participate

Probable Fields of Question:
  1. Verbal and Non-Verbal Reasoning.
  2. Mathematical Reasoning and Quantitative Aptitude.
  3. Straight Lines, Circles, Conic Sections .
  4. Probability.
  5. Statistics .
  6. Trigonometry.
  7. Limits and Derivatives.
  8. Complex Numbers & Quadratic Equations.
  9. Linear Inequations.

  1. A team can have 1 - 3 members.
  2. Each team must have a Team Name Team members can be from any college or university.

  1. There will be 2 Divisions for the Project Showcase.
    1. Unior Division – For College Students.
    2. Senior Division – For Undergraduate University Students.
  2. IAll projects must be either software or hardware.
  3. Every participant and the participating team may bring a poster describing their project. Teams must showcase a demo of their project in action. Each participant or team will have to present their project to the judges.
  4. The winners will be determined by a panel of Judges. The Judges’ decision is final. The top two projects will be the Winner and Runner-Up.
  5. Each participant is responsible for the safety and security of their project and belongings. The organizing committee will not be held responsible for the loss of any equipment or belongings.
